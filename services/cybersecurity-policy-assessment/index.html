Who it is for

A focused engagement for a defined security decision.

Organizations preparing for audits, formalizing governance, updating outdated documents, or resolving gaps between policy and practice.

01

Policies have not kept pace with technology or roles

02

Audit findings cite documentation or governance gaps

03

Security ownership and exceptions are unclear

04

Multiple documents conflict or duplicate requirements

Assessment coverage

Coverage shaped around the approved objective.

Testing never extends beyond explicitly authorized systems, facilities, methods, identities, locations, or time windows.

What may be assessed

Approved coverage.

  • Policy inventory and hierarchy
  • Roles, approvals, and exception handling
  • Alignment to selected frameworks or obligations
  • Operational evidence and stakeholder interviews where included
Expected deliverables

Evidence designed to support action.

  • Gap and overlap analysis
  • Prioritized policy recommendations
  • Ownership and review-cycle guidance
  • Roadmap for document updates
Methodology

Structured, authorized, and grounded in business context.

The process adapts to the selected service and environment. No active testing begins without an agreed scope and rules of engagement.

01

Discover

Understand the environment
Clarify objectives, stakeholders, constraints, assets, and authorization.

02

Assess

Evaluate the exposure
Apply the approved methods and collect proportionate, defensible evidence.

03

Prioritize

Connect findings to impact
Consider severity, exploitability, business context, urgency, and effort.

04

Remediate

Build a practical roadmap
Translate findings into owned, sequenced, and measurable actions.

05

Validate

Confirm improvement
Where included, retest eligible findings and document remaining risk.
