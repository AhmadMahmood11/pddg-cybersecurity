Engagement process

A five-stage path from uncertainty to accountable action.

Each stage connects technical evidence to a clear decision, responsible owner, and measurable next action.

01

Discover

Understand the environment
Clarify objectives, stakeholders, constraints, assets, and authorization.

02

Assess

Evaluate the exposure
Apply the approved methods and collect proportionate, defensible evidence.

03

Prioritize

Connect findings to impact
Consider severity, exploitability, business context, urgency, and effort.

04

Remediate

Build a practical roadmap
Translate findings into owned, sequenced, and measurable actions.

05

Validate

Confirm improvement
Where included, retest eligible findings and document remaining risk.

Reporting audiences

One evidence base. Two decision views.

Technical and executive audiences need different framing without losing traceability to the underlying evidence.

Technical teams

Reproduce, remediate, and validate.

  • Affected assets and conditions
  • Reproducible technical evidence
  • Practical remediation guidance
  • Validation and retest status
Executive teams

Understand material exposure and ownership.

  • Business and operational impact
  • Priority and urgency context
  • Accountable ownership
  • Remaining risk and next decision
Evidence handling

Collect only what is necessary to support the finding.

Evidence practices are established during scoping and may include minimization, redaction, approved storage, secure transfer, access controls, retention periods, and destruction expectations.

Minimize

Limit sensitive information.

Collect proportionate evidence and avoid unnecessary personal, regulated, or operational data.

Protect

Control storage and transfer.

Use agreed channels, access restrictions, handling expectations, and retention periods.

Explain

State limitations clearly.

Document assumptions, exclusions, constraints, and what the assessment does not establish.
