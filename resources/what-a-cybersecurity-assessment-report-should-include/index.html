Use this guide to evaluate whether a report can support both technical remediation and leadership decisions.

Executive context

A useful report explains why the assessment occurred, what was in scope, the material themes, and the decisions leadership should consider.

Technical evidence

Findings should identify affected assets, conditions, evidence, impact, and enough detail for responsible teams to reproduce and remediate the issue.

Prioritization and ownership

Technical severity alone is not enough. Reports should consider exploitability, business impact, exposure, compensating controls, urgency, effort, ownership, and validation expectations.

Limitations and remaining risk

A credible report states assumptions, exclusions, testing constraints, evidence-handling boundaries, and what the assessment does not prove.

This resource provides general educational information. Scope, authorization, testing methods, evidence, and reporting should be tailored to the organization and engagement.
