Move beyond a severity-only list by connecting technical evidence to exploitability, operational context, ownership, and urgency.

Severity is one input

A high technical score does not automatically mean the highest business priority. Asset importance, exposure, realistic attack paths, compensating controls, and operational consequences change the decision.

Use a consistent model

Consider technical severity, likelihood and exploitability, business impact, affected data and operations, exposure, remediation effort, dependencies, and accountable ownership.

Make the output actionable

The final priority should support a clear next action, named owner, target timing, exception path, and validation method.

This resource provides general educational information. Scope, authorization, testing methods, evidence, and reporting should be tailored to the organization and engagement.
